Program Purpose

Build a practical Enterprise Architecture capability that connects business strategy, capabilities, platforms, data, integration, and technology investments into a clear transformation direction.

Why it matters

Enterprise Architecture helps organizations reduce complexity, prevent fragmented decisions, and ensure technology investments support the business capabilities that matter most.

Common Challenge

Technology decisions are often made project by project, creating duplicated platforms, inconsistent standards, unclear ownership, and limited visibility into the future-state architecture.

Business Impact

Complexity increases cost, slows delivery, creates integration friction, and makes it harder for leaders to prioritize investments with confidence.

Program Outcome

A business-aligned architecture model that clarifies the current state, defines the target state, and guides transformation through standards, roadmaps, and governance.

What the program helps you build

Core Capabilities

  • Business capability mapping connected to applications, data, platforms, integrations, and investments.
  • Current-state and target-state architecture views that make complexity visible and actionable.
  • Architecture principles, standards, reference models, and decision guardrails.
  • Roadmaps, governance routines, and architecture review processes that align execution with strategy.

Client Value

  • Improved visibility into technology complexity, redundancy, risk, and modernization priorities.
  • Stronger alignment between business strategy, investment decisions, and technology execution.
  • Reduced duplication through reuse, standardization, and clearer platform direction.
  • Faster decision-making through architecture principles, governance, and executive-ready roadmaps.

Transformation journey

The program can start with a focused architecture assessment and evolve into an operating capability that continuously guides modernization, simplification, and business alignment.

1. AssessReview business priorities, capability needs, application landscape, data flows, integrations, platforms, and known pain points.
2. MapCreate current-state views that connect capabilities, systems, data, integrations, ownership, cost, risk, and business value.
3. AlignAlign business and technology leaders on architecture principles, target outcomes, decision rights, and modernization priorities.
4. DesignDefine target-state architecture, reference models, standards, platform direction, and simplification opportunities.
5. PrioritizeTranslate architecture gaps into initiatives based on business value, complexity reduction, risk, dependency, and feasibility.
6. RoadmapCreate a phased architecture roadmap that connects initiatives, dependencies, milestones, investment needs, and expected outcomes.
7. GovernLaunch architecture review routines, decision forums, exception management, and standards adoption across delivery teams.
8. ImproveContinuously refresh architecture views, standards, and roadmaps as business priorities and technology conditions evolve.
