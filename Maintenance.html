Application Maintenance & Support

Stabilize critical applications, reduce operational noise, and transform support from reactive ticket handling into a disciplined service model that protects business continuity and improves over time.

Explore the program View outcomes

Why it matters

Many organizations depend on applications that have grown complex, fragile, and difficult to support. Incidents take too long to resolve, knowledge is scattered, ownership is unclear, and delivery teams are repeatedly pulled away from strategic work.

The Application Maintenance & Support program creates the operating discipline needed to keep applications reliable, supportable, and aligned to business priorities.

Client challenge

When support is reactive, application issues become business issues. Service interruptions, recurring defects, slow response times, and unclear escalation paths reduce confidence in IT and limit the organization’s ability to move faster.

Business outcomes

Greater stability

Improve availability, reliability, and service continuity across business-critical applications.

Faster resolution

Reduce incident response and recovery time through better triage, ownership, and escalation models.

Lower support noise

Identify recurring issues, eliminate root causes, and reduce avoidable operational demand.

Continuous improvement

Use support insights to improve applications, processes, documentation, and user experience.

What the program delivers

  • Application support model and service ownership structure.
  • Incident, problem, request, and escalation management practices.
  • SLA, KPI, and operational dashboard definitions.
  • Knowledge management and support documentation standards.
  • Root cause analysis and recurring issue reduction model.
  • Backlog, enhancement, and technical debt prioritization approach.

Who it helps

This program is designed for CIOs, IT operations leaders, application owners, product teams, service managers, and business stakeholders who need dependable applications and a support model that scales with demand.

It is especially relevant when application support consumes too much delivery capacity, service levels are inconsistent, or business teams lack confidence in operational responsiveness.

Program journey

A practical path to move from reactive support to managed, measurable, and continuously improving application services.

Assess

Review application criticality, ticket trends, service performance, ownership, and current support practices.

Prioritize

Identify the applications, incidents, pain points, and operational risks that require immediate attention.

Design

Define the support model, roles, SLAs, escalation paths, knowledge standards, and improvement cadence.

Stabilize

Strengthen incident management, reduce recurring issues, and improve service reliability.

Industrialize

Standardize support workflows, dashboards, documentation, and governance routines.

Automate

Introduce monitoring, alerting, self-service, workflow automation, and AI-assisted support where appropriate.

Optimize

Use operational insights to reduce technical debt, enhance applications, and improve user experience.

Scale

Expand the model across the application portfolio with consistent metrics and service expectations.

Key performance indicators

Incident reduction

Lower recurring incidents and avoidable support demand.

SLA performance

Improve response, resolution, and service-level attainment.

Application health

Increase stability, availability, and operational confidence.

Proof of value

A focused MVP can target a selected set of critical applications to validate the support model, define service metrics, reduce recurring issues, and establish an improvement backlog.

Expected MVP outputs

  • Application support assessment.
  • Target operating model for support.
  • SLA and KPI dashboard design.
  • Top recurring issues and root-cause actions.
  • Knowledge management starter pack.
  • 90-day stabilization roadmap.
