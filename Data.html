Data Architecture

Establish the data structures, standards, governance, and integration patterns required to create trusted information, reduce data fragmentation, and enable better decisions across the enterprise.

Data Strategy Information Models Data Governance Analytics Readiness

Program Purpose

Build a practical Data Architecture capability that connects business capabilities, systems, integrations, analytics, and governance into a trusted data foundation for transformation.

Why it matters

Data Architecture helps organizations move from fragmented, inconsistent, and hard-to-trust data toward a clear enterprise model that supports operations, reporting, automation, AI, and executive decision-making.

Common Challenge

Data is often duplicated across systems, defined differently by teams, moved through fragile integrations, and governed inconsistently across business and technology ownership.

Business Impact

Inconsistent data slows decisions, weakens reporting confidence, increases reconciliation work, limits automation, and creates risk when transformation programs depend on unreliable information.

Program Outcome

A clear data architecture model with trusted domains, data flows, ownership, standards, quality routines, and a roadmap to improve analytics and operational intelligence.

What the program helps you build

Core Capabilities

  • Enterprise data domain mapping connected to business capabilities, applications, integrations, analytics, and ownership.
  • Current-state and target-state data views that clarify master data, transactional data, reference data, reporting data, and data flows.
  • Data standards, information models, quality rules, governance routines, stewardship roles, and data decision rights.
  • Roadmaps that prioritize data modernization, integration simplification, reporting confidence, and analytics enablement.

Client Value

  • Improved trust in operational, financial, customer, product, supplier, and performance information.
  • Reduced manual reconciliation and reporting friction through clearer definitions, ownership, and data quality routines.
  • Stronger foundation for analytics, automation, AI, regulatory reporting, and executive performance management.
  • Better transformation decisions through visible dependencies between data, systems, processes, and business outcomes.

Transformation journey

The program can begin with a targeted data assessment and evolve into an operating capability that continuously improves data trust, usability, governance, and analytics readiness.

1. AssessReview business priorities, data pain points, critical reports, systems of record, integrations, ownership, quality issues, and analytics needs.
2. MapCreate current-state views of data domains, master data, data flows, reporting dependencies, integration points, lineage, and known quality gaps.
3. AlignAlign business and technology leaders on critical data domains, definitions, ownership, decision rights, governance needs, and improvement priorities.
4. DesignDefine target-state data architecture, domain models, data standards, stewardship model, integration principles, and quality management approach.
5. PrioritizeTranslate data gaps into initiatives based on business value, reporting impact, process dependency, regulatory need, feasibility, and transformation risk.
6. RoadmapCreate a phased data roadmap that sequences governance, quality, integration, master data, reporting, and analytics improvements.
7. GovernLaunch data governance routines, ownership forums, data quality reviews, issue resolution processes, and standards adoption across teams.
8. ImproveContinuously refresh data models, quality rules, ownership, lineage, and roadmap priorities as business needs and systems evolve.
