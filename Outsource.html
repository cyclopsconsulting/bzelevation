Why this program matters

Many IT organizations are expected to deliver more with constrained budgets, scarce talent, fragmented vendors, and uneven service performance. Outsourcing Solutions helps clients design, govern, and operate a fit-for-purpose sourcing model that increases capacity without losing accountability.

Capacity constraints

Teams need flexible access to skills and scale without overextending internal resources or creating unsustainable delivery pressure.

Vendor fragmentation

Multiple providers, unclear ownership, and inconsistent practices make performance hard to manage and outcomes difficult to compare.

Service inconsistency

Without clear SLAs, governance, metrics, and operating routines, outsourced work can become reactive, opaque, and misaligned to business priorities.

Program purpose

Outsourcing Solutions creates a structured sourcing and managed services model that aligns work allocation, service ownership, delivery governance, financial transparency, and continuous improvement.

Define the model

Clarify which capabilities should be retained, partnered, outsourced, or scaled through managed services.

Improve accountability

Establish ownership, decision rights, SLAs, KPIs, escalation paths, and performance routines.

Optimize cost and value

Connect sourcing decisions to business outcomes, service quality, productivity, and cost transparency.

Scale with confidence

Enable flexible capacity, specialized skills, and resilient operations across projects, products, and run services.

Eight-step transformation journey

The journey moves from sourcing clarity to operational control, ensuring outsourced capabilities are designed around measurable performance and business value.

Assess demand

Understand workload, skill gaps, service pain points, and capacity needs.

Segment services

Classify work by strategic value, complexity, risk, and sourcing suitability.

Define model

Design the retained, partnered, outsourced, and managed services structure.

Set governance

Establish ownership, routines, decision rights, and escalation paths.

Build scorecards

Define SLAs, KPIs, performance dashboards, and business outcome measures.

Transition services

Plan onboarding, knowledge transfer, stabilization, and risk controls.

Operate and improve

Manage performance, resolve issues, optimize costs, and improve quality.

Scale the model

Expand successful practices across programs, products, and operating domains.
